Project Idea and Goals

This project includes a thematic chloropleth map which displays the number of illegal waste dumpings reported across census tracts in Seattle. The map uses a color scale to represent the different levels of equity priority across census tracts as well. By mapping the number of illegal dumping reports alongside equity priority across these census tracts we are able to identify a trend using a bar chart. Areas with higher counts of illegal dumping generally have a higher level of equity priority. The equity priority is determined by a lot of different features such as race, ethnicity, and related demographics with data on socioeconomic and health disadvantages. Overall, the goal of this project is to be able to visualize a clear understanding of the illegal dumping in Seattle and to understand how these dumpings have affected the level of racial and social equity of people across the city of Seattle.
